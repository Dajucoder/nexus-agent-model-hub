# License Guide

## Default License

This repository ships under **PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0**.

Official source:

- https://polyformproject.org/licenses/noncommercial/1.0.0/
- https://polyformproject.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/PolyForm-Noncommercial-1.0.0.txt

## What It Means In Practice

Allowed without a separate commercial agreement:

- Personal study
- Research and experimentation
- Internal testing for noncommercial purposes
- Use by educational, charitable, governmental, and public research organizations

Not allowed under the default license:

- Selling this software
- Offering it as a paid or revenue-supporting hosted service
- Embedding it into a commercial SaaS or on-prem product
- Running it inside a for-profit commercial operation for business advantage
- Delivering managed hosting to paying clients

The last three bullets are operational interpretations of the official noncommercial limitation and are provided for clarity, not legal advice.

## Why PolyForm Noncommercial Was Chosen

- It matches the repository owner's requirement for strict noncommercial boundaries.
- It avoids the inaccurate claim that AGPL prohibits commercial use.
- It makes hosted-service restrictions clearer for operators and contributors.

## Comparison With Common Licenses

- `MIT` and `Apache-2.0` allow commercial use.
- `AGPL-3.0` requires source disclosure for networked modified versions, but still allows commercial use.
- `SSPL` focuses on service-source disclosure obligations, not a general noncommercial ban.
- `PolyForm Noncommercial` is stricter for business use because only noncommercial purposes are permitted by default.

## If You Need Commercial Rights

Add a separate commercial license offer outside this repository and point users to it from the root `README`.
